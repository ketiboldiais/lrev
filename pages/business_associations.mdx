# Business Associations

## Agency Relationships
- agency is the ~fiduciary relationship~ that exists between an agent and principal where the agent acts on the principal's behalf and is subject to the principal's control.

### Creation of Agency
- an agency relationship is created when:
	1. [ ] the parties voluntarily consent (express or implied) to enter into an agency relationship, and
	2. [ ] the agent is subject to the principal's control
		- e.g., supervision is sufficient.[^control_note]
[^control_note]: The amount of control may be limited.

### Termination
- There are two ways to terminate an agency relationship:
	1. termination by the parties themselves, or
	2. termination by law
- an agency relationship is terminated by the parties if:
	- [ ] the agent or principal indicates to the other
	- [ ] the desire to terminate the agency relationship,
	<OR/>
	- [ ] the express terms of the agency expire
	<OR/>
	- [ ] the purpose of the agency relationship is fulfilled
- an agency relationship is termined by law if:
	- [ ] the agent or principal dies
		- some jurisdictions require that the party receive notice of the other's death,
	<OR/>
	- [ ] the agent or principal loses capacity
		- some jurisdictions require that the party receive notice of the other's incapacity
	<OR/>
	- [ ] the agent materially breaches a fiduciary duty owed to the principal

- Once a principal is validly bound to a contract by their agent, they're liable under the contract's terms.
- Agents can bind a principal to a contract if:
	1. [ ] the agent is acting within their ~actual apparent authority~,
	<OR/>
	2. [ ] the agent acts within their inherent agency power.

### Actual Authority
- An agent acts with actual authority when:
	1. [ ] at the time of taking action that
	2. [ ] has legal consequences for the principal,  
	3. [ ] the agent:
		- [ ] reasonably believes,
		- [ ] according to the principal's manifestations,
		- [ ] the princpal wants the agent to act
- Actual authority can be express or implied

#### Actual Express Authority
- Actual express authority exists when the principal directs the agent to engage in the precise task in question.

#### Actual Implied Authority
- Actual implied authority exists when the agent believes, based on a reasonable interpretation of the principal's words or conduct, that principal wishes him (the agent) to act on the principal's behalf.

##### Incidental Authority  
- The agent's authority to conduct a transaction includes the authority to engage in actions that are incidental to it.

### Apparent Authority
- an agent acts with apparent authority when:
	1. [ ] the principal holds the agent out as having authority to act on the principal's behalf, and
	2. [ ] the principal's conduct, when reasonably interpreted, causes a third party to rely on the agent's appearance of authority when dealing with the agent.
- ~DEFENSE~:
	- apparent authority __does not__ exist if the third party has knowledge that the agent does not have actual authority.

### Inherent Agency Power
- Under equity, the doctrine of ~inherent agency power~ allows courts to hold a principal liable for damages to third parties even if the principal's agent acted without actual or apparent authority.
- The doctrine applies when:
	1. [ ] an agency relationship exists, <AND/>
	2. [ ] the totality of circumstances weighs against forcing the third party to absorb all of the damages

#### Undisclosed Principals
- Courts commonly apply the inherent agency power to hold a principal liable for their agent's unauthorized actions when the principal is undisclosed to the third party.
- Note that an agent for an undisclosed principal cannot have apparent authority because the principal cannot hold the agent out as having authority to a third party if the third party is unaware of the principal.

## Principal's Vicarious Liability for Agent Acts

### Principal's Tort Liability
- Under the ~respondeat superior~, an employer (principal) may be liable for torts committed by an employee (agent) if:
	1. [ ] __Relationship Existence__. an employer-employee relationship exists (not an independent) contractor relationship, <AND/>
	2. [ ] __Employment Scope__. the employee's commission of the tort occurs within the scope of employment.
