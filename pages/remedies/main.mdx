## Compensatory Damages
- Def: an award of money to compensate the plaintiff for loss or injury.
- Requirements for an award:
	1. [ ] the plaintiff's injury must have a cause
	2. [ ] the plaintiff's injury must have a cause
	3. [ ] the plaintiff's injury must have been certain to occur
	4. [ ] the plaintiff's injury must have been unavoidable
	5. [ ] for the plaintiff's injuries that could or could not have been avoided, damages are limited to those that could not have been reasonably avoided, under the _consequences doctrine_.
- There are two kinds: _general damages_ and _special damages_.

### General Damages
- Def: An award of money for losses that are foreseeable
	- Example: pain and suffering
- Traits:
	- General damages do not have to be specifically pleaded

### Special Damages
- Def: An award of money for losses that are not necessarily foreseeable
	- Example: Lost wages, lost profits
- Traits:
	- Special damages must be specifically pleaded

## Nominal Damages
- Def: Damages awarded even if the plaintiff sustains no actual injury.
- For injuries where actual injury is a required element (e.g., fraud), nominal damages cannot be awarded.

## Punitive Damages
- Def: Damages areded to punish a defendant for "willful, wanton, or malicious conduct."
- Traits:
	- Generally limited to intentioanl torts, although they may also be recoverable in cases of reckless misconduct.
	- Some courts require a reasonable relationship between punitive and compensatory damages awards.
