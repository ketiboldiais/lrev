<Quiz title="Assault 1">

- A host pointed an unloaded revolver at her guest, threatening to shoot him.
- The guest knew that the revolver was not loaded.
- The question also knew that the ammunition for the revolver was stored in a locked basement closet, two stories below where the two were then standing.
- In an action brought by the guest against the host for assault, will the guest be likely to prevail?

1. No, because the host did not intend to shoot her guest.
	- Incorrect.
	- This answer correctly states that the guest will likely lose, but it misstates the legal basis for this conclusion.
	- Even though the host did not intend to shoot the guest, she may well have intended to cause the guest to fear being shot.
	- If she had succeeded in creating that fear, the guest would have a viable assault claim against her.

2. No, because the host did not put her guest in apprehension of an imminent contact.
	- Correct.
	- The tort of assault requires that the plaintiff have an apprehension of an imminent harmful or offensive bodily contact.
	- That result did not occur here, because the guest knew that the revolver was not loaded and that the ammunition was in a locked basement closet.

3. Yes, because the ammunition was accessible to the host.
	- Incorrect.
	- Assault requires that the bodily contact apprehended be imminent.
	- It would take the host some time to retrieve the ammunition from a locked closet two stories below, so the guest had no reasonable fear of imminent harmful or offensive contact.

4. Yes, because the host threatened her guest with a revolver.
	- Incorrect.
	- A threat is not enough to support a case for assault unless it actually results in an apprehension of immediate harmful or offensive bodily contact.
	- In this case, the guest knew that the revolver was not loaded.
</Quiz>
