# Civil Procedure

## Personal Jurisdiction

- ~Personal jurisdiction~ := the court's authority to render a decision binding
  the parties appearing before it.
  > General rule: A federal court has personal jurisdiction if the defendant
  > could be subjected to the jurisdiction of the courts of the state where the
  > federal district court is located. <FRCP section={'4(k)(1)(A)'}/>.

- There are two types of long-arm statutes:
  1.  General long-arm statutes, and
  2.  Enumerated long-arm statutes.
- General long-arm statutes provide personal jurisdiction to the extent its
  permitted under the federal constitution. Thus, if a state provides an
  implicit long-arm statute, the court jumps straight to the constitutional
  analysis for determining personal jurisdiction.
  - Examples: California and Rhode Island's long-arm statutes.
- Enumerated long-arm statutes explicitly provide the necessary conditions for
  personal jurisdiction.
  - Examples: New York's long-arm statute.

### Diversity Jurisdiction

- ~Diversity jurisdiction~ := subject matter jurisdiction provided by <USC
  title={'28'} section={'1332'}/>.
- Diversity jurisdiction arose from the historical concern of protecting
  out-of-state parties from local prejudice.
