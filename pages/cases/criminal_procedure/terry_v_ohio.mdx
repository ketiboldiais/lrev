# Facts

- Terry was sentenced to 1-3 years for carrying a concealed weapon.
- A patrolling officer in plain clothes saw Terry and another man walking up and down the street, peering into windows.
- After seeing the two repeat their behavior multiple times, the officer grabbed Terry and patted him down.
- The officer felt a pistol, pulled it out, and subsequently arrested Terry.

# Issue

- Whether the pistol's admission in evidence violated Terry's rights under the 4th Amendment.

# Analysis

- Whenever a police officer restrains an individual's freedom, that individual has been **seized**.
- The 4th Amendment protects against _unreasonable_ searches and seizures.
- To determine if a search and seizure is reasonable, courts must consider two factors:
	1.  whether the officer's action$as justified at its inception, and
	2.  whether it was reasonably related in scope to the circumstances which justified the interference in the first place.
