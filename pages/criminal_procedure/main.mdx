Criminal procedure is the body law governing how the prosecution, defense, and judgments of crime are conducted.

## 4th Amendment
The fourth amendment governs searches and seizures. The amendment provides:

> The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized.

### Searches
- A __search__ is:
	1. [ ] any government conduct that
	2. [ ] violates a person's [reasonable expectation of privacy](#reasonable-expectation-of-privacy)
- Under the 4th Amendment, a person is protected from unreasonable government searches. This protection is provided through the [warrant clause](#the-warrant-clause).

#### Reasonable Expectation of Privacy
- Places where a reasonable expectation of privacy exists include:
  - homes
  - hotel rooms
  - offices
  - a home's backyard (curtilage)
  - luggage
- Places where there is NO reasonable expectation of privacy
  - public streets
  - open fields (even if the open field is private property)
  - garbage left out in the street
  - abandoned property
  - anything visible from public airspace
  - anything that can be seen inside one's home from public space

#### The Warrant Clause
- By default, the government needs a valid warrant to conduct a [search](#searches).
- A warrant is valid only if it satisfies the following conditions:
	1. [ ] __Issuance__. The warrant is issued by a neutral magistrate,
	2. [ ] __Probable Cause__. The warrant is issued based on probable cause to believe that the items sought are fruits, instrumentalities, or evidence of crime, <AND/>
	3. [ ] __Description.__ The warrant describes the place and property to be searched with peculariaty
- If the government conducts a search without a valid warrant, then the government must demonstrate that an [an exception to the warrant clause applies](#exceptions-to-the-warrant-clause).
- If the government conducts a search
	1. [ ] without a valid warrant, and
	2. [ ] cannot demonstrate an exception to the warrant clause,
  - => evidence obtained from the search will be excluded from the State's case-in-chief.

#### Exceptions to the Warrant Clause
- The government does not need a warrant to conduct a [search](#searches) if any of the following conditions are true:
  - [ ] the [doctrine of exigent circumstances](#exigent-circumstances) applies,
  - [ ] the search is incident to lawful arrest,
  - [ ] the searchee consented to the search,
  - [ ] the search is done to an automobile,
  - [ ] the doctrine of plain view applies,
  - [ ] the search is administrative
  - [ ] the search is a stop and frisk
- A helpful acronym: ESCAPES (Exigent circumtances, Search incident to lawful arrest, Consent, Automobiles, Plain view, Evidence from administrative searches, Stop and frisk).
